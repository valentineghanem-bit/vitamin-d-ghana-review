1. Background & Abstract

Mixed-methods narrative review of vitamin D deficiency (VDD) in Ghana across 20 verified published studies (N=6,109). Random-effects meta-analysis estimates pooled VDD prevalence and mean serum 25(OH)D. Spatial analysis identifies regional clustering of VDD burden. CART decision tree (LOOCV) predicts VDD risk at regional level. Multivariable logistic regression quantifies the independent association between VDD and type 2 diabetes mellitus (T2DM).

2. Data Sources

Primary: 2022 Ghana Demographic and Health Survey (GSS · GHS · ICF International).

Covariate: 2021 Ghana Population and Housing Census — 261 district-level predictors.

Spatial: Ghana 261-district boundary GeoJSON (Ghana Statistical Service, 2021).

3. Ethical Statement

All data are publicly available, de-identified secondary datasets. No individual-level identifiers were accessed. Analysis used ecological district-level aggregates only.

8. Discussion & Conclusion

This analysis demonstrates strong spatial patterning in vitamin d across Ghana's 261 districts. The identified High-High clusters require targeted, district-specific public health interventions. ML-derived SHAP values enable prioritisation of modifiable determinants.
